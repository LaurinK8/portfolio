Android Logging App

Overview

I built a lightweight Android logging tool in Android Studio (Java) for dormakaba’s time‑recording terminals. The app runs as a background service and periodically polls the terminal’s power‑supply interfaces to capture voltage and current readings, while also listening to the device’s built‑in accelerometer for vibration or shock events.

All measurements are timestamped and appended to plain‑text log files on the device’s storage, with a simple, human‑readable format (CSV‑style) that service technicians can access via USB or Wi‑Fi. By avoiding complex UIs or databases, the solution remains resource‑light and easy to deploy across terminals. Technically, it leverages Android’s SensorManager and hardware I/O APIs, thread‑safe scheduling (Handler/AlarmManager), and robust file I/O with appropriate permission handling.

This straightforward logging approach empowers field technicians to review power‑stability trends and mechanical disturbances post‑service, speeding up fault diagnosis and reducing on‑site troubleshooting time.
